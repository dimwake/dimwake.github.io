The Paper Story

  1. July 13, 2025
  2. 2 minutes

We all use paper.

We write on it, fold it, scribble our thoughts when no one’s watching.
Sometimes, we use it to clean messes. Other times, we pour our poetry onto it.
But never do we ask the paper if it’s ready.

It never resists.

Some days, we crush it in frustration.
Not because it did anything wrong—
but because we couldn’t control what we were feeling.

And when the storm passes, we open it again.
It’s still there. Quiet.
But now, it holds wrinkles.
Faint lines from where our anger touched it.
Black creases—memories of being crumpled and discarded.

Yet it stays useful.
Still holds our words, our sketches, our tears.

And maybe—just maybe—the paper wasn’t perfect.

Maybe it smudged the ink.
Maybe it let the words fade.
Maybe it tore a little at the corners on its own.

But isn’t that part of being real?

Flawed, yes. But still willing.
Still trying.
Still waiting to be held with softness.

But not every hand learns to be soft.
Some only know to press, to fold, to demand.

And the more we crush it—
the more we try to force it to carry what we can’t carry ourselves—
the more it loses what it once was.

Until one day, the paper tears.
Not loudly.
Not out of anger.
Just… quietly.

Because even silence has its limits.

And we’re left staring,
not at a piece of paper—
but at the proof
that even the kindest things
can wear thin.
